The past month has brought a vast amount of changes. To begin, the team was introduced to a number of new concepts, and ideas by our teaching team; not only from the NYC design factory but from various external design factories as well. To begin with, we were introduced to Planet Centric Design as a means for us to understand the effect our system has on individual ‚Äúearthly‚Äù resources. The teaching team wanted us to understand how each of our systems would affect earth. This activity involved a visiting team posting questions on resource consumption, forcing us to think of ways we could automate certain tasks. In particular, one of the stages in the system involved the use of people collecting and transporting algae to a processing unit which has since been automated.

The other activity that was of great use to our team was Technology deep dives. This activity introduced us to a few resources around emerging technology and identifying development cycles using the Gartner hype cycle. This activity also involved prompts about identifying any emerging (or Deep tech from CERN) that the system could make use of.

These resources and prompts provided the team with enough motivation to think about how technology might evolve in the next 25 years, considering evolution over the last 25 years. In addition, the team also made use of Gartner's hype cycle to predict feasibility upon identifying potential use cases from the Tech Trends report. With research in each of these fields inspiring us, we started working on sketches of how the system and its components (particularly the sensors) might look and work. After many iterations of white boarding and sketching, we started to form an idea on how the system would operate.

However, something our team is worried about is, despite there being a significant amount of research on each of these technologies independently, none of them have been field tested extensively enough for us to be able to predict either requirements or outputs with some of them.
